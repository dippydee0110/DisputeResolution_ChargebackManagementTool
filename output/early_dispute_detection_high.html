Suggested Agent Recommendations

  • Ask for order confirmation and delivery tracking
    Confirm whether the customer received the item, when it was scheduled, and whether the tracking data matches the merchant’s delivery details.
  • Request merchant communications
    Obtain email or message threads showing customer contact with the merchant regarding the service or shipment.
  • Validate authorization and customer signature
    If available, secure proof that the purchase was authorized and that the cardholder was aware the charge occurred.

How the Agent Should Respond

When customer provides order/shipping evidence:

Confirm the exact delivery date and compare it to the dispute date. If the delivery timeline supports the merchant’s claim, prepare evidence for representment and note that the customer is cooperating.

When customer has only partial evidence:

Ask for additional detail such as invoice numbers, payment method, and communication with the merchant. Use the partial evidence to build a stronger narrative for the chargeback defense.

When amount is high and evidence is weak:

Escalate to senior support or representment team immediately. Advise the customer that stronger documentation is critical to reduce financial risk.

Evidence to Request

  • Proof of delivery or receipt
    Delivery confirmation, signed receipt, or tracking updates from the merchant.
  • Order invoice or confirmation email
    Original sales receipt and order summary showing product details and price.
  • Customer complaint or refund request history
    Any communications showing the customer disputed the service before filing a chargeback.
